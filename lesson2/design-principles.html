Visual Heirarchy

Visual hierarchy focuses on arranging elements to show their order of importance. Designers structure visual characteristics so users can understand information easily. By laying out elements strategically, designers influence users' perceptions and guide them to desired actions. For example, it is fairly universal that the symbol of three stacked horizontal lines represents a hidden menu that users can click or tap to access.

Chase's website before signing in has it's biggest products front and center because Chase knows that if you don't have a login already, you are a potential new customer who is linkely looking for a bank account, credit card, or mortgage.

Hick's Law

Hick's Law states that users take longer to make a decision as the number of options increases. Take for example the Charles Schwab mobile site. With so many different routes to take, as a user I would likely feel overwhelmed with the ammount of links on their homepage. By nature of the services it provides, Schwab may not be able to easily compartmentalize it's services into large categories. However, if it had more finger friendly navigation it would have been a more pleasant experience.

Since Schwab has so many options for investment accounts, they seek to funnel potential customers to the right page depending on the questions they answer. Where Chase assumes customers know who they are and what they offer, Schwab's design seeks to make a good first impression with a stranger.

Charles Schwab Mobile Site

Rule of Thirds

This is a guideline that proposes an image should be visually divided into nine equal parts by two equally spaced horizontal lines and two equally spaced vertical lines, and that important compositional elements should be placed along these lines or their intersections. I looked for a website that best exemplified this rule and found Amazon's homepage to be a good example.

While the homepage isn't split evenly like the lines that show up on your phone's camera, the homepage is designed in such a way that there is an easily understandable symmetry to the website.
