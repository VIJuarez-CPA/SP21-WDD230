Visual Heirarchy

Visual hierarchy focuses on arranging elements to show their order of importance. Designers structure visual characteristics so users can understand information easily. By laying out elements strategically, designers influence users' perceptions and guide them to desired actions. For example, it is fairly universal that the symbol of three stacked horizontal lines represents a hidden menu that users can click or tap to access.
